Student abroad dispatch

Collaborative research beyond national borders is very active in Europe and the United States (especially in Europe), and it is greatly linked to the development potential of research.
Although Japan is disadvantageous in terms of distance, I think that international collaborative research will become more and more important from now on in order to compete with the world's most advanced, and actively proceed.

Everything is not officially subsidized, but fortunately, five collaborative researches are currently in progress, and students are also active in participating in this collaborative research depending on the theme.

Depending on research progress, not only our staff but also our students (even in 4th graders!) Are actively promoting short-term collaborative research for about two weeks going abroad.

Fortunately, the Institute for Resources and Chemistry is an advanced research institute that will support overseas research presentations and collaborative research.
Since 2006, a large number of students have gone abroad to participate in collaborative research.

Collaborative research abroad, as opposed to going abroad by sightseeing trips or language training, will proceed while discussing with the world's top line-class researchers and their students on the same research theme, so if there is no such practical school think. Even if you are not good at English, you have to go, and it will be fine well outside and there is a common interest, so you can get along well soon.
